Understanding Your Bingo Card

Your bingo card is populated with writerly moves that define the op-ed genre.

Not every op-ed makes all of these moves, but most can be found in most op-eds. These are the building blocks of effective opinion writing!

How the Game Works

Step 1: Your professor will call out a specific rhetorical move from your bingo card.

Step 2: You'll have a limited amount of time to locate a passage in one of the provided op-eds that makes that move.

Step 3: Once you find an example, highlight it and tag it with the corresponding move!

Highlighting & Tagging Passages

Switch to the "Sample Op-Eds" tab and open any article. When you find a passage that demonstrates a rhetorical move:

  • Highlight the text by selecting it with your mouse
  • Click the highlighted text to see a menu of all rhetorical moves
  • Select the move that matches your passage

üí° Tip: Keep your selections brief but meaningful (usually 1-2 sentences)!

Review & Save Your Entry

After selecting a move, you'll automatically see a dialog box with:

  • Your highlighted passage - already filled in for you
  • Source information - bibliographical data automatically added

Review the entry and click "Save" to mark the square on your bingo card. You can always click a square later to view or edit what you've saved!

The Purpose

This game helps you:

  • Familiarize yourself with the op-ed genre
  • Understand the rhetorical moves that define this genre
  • Practice identifying these moves in real-world context
